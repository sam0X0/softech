Accounting

Some of the reasons why businesses maintain books of accounts:

  • To fulfill legal requirements – records as per standards and practices
  • To pay taxes to various bodies – statutory needs
  • Keep the business running – manage receivables & payables, cash, bank etc
  • Keep a watch on the pulse of the organization – key performance indicators
  • Respond to queries – to locate the right transaction immediately

Tally software has been perfecting this ‘simple’ recording method for over twenty years. All this is in the quest to deliver better, faster, and more flexible ways to fulfill your needs in the true sense and not just maintain books of accounts. A few examples will illustrate:

  • All books or accounts, reports, all totals, and sub-totals are instantly updated when you enter a voucher (this is what we call all accounting transactions). There is nothing more that needs to be done – whether you are inserting a forgotten entry, or correcting one.
  • Specific taxation requirements of all the states of India wherever applicable are available in Tally software, in the prescribed formats
  • You can carry out all supporting tasks like reminder letters, delayed interest, ageing, bank reconciliation, etc
  • MIS to look at all important business ratios
  • Drill down from any report, even the Balance Sheet, right to any voucher or filter and search result.
  • Data entry in the language of the users choice – and report in any other language (from the languages supported)
  • Mark vouchers that are draft as ‘optional’ and convert these to the final status- with a single button click

Financial Management

Tally ERP 9 – Your Lifeline | Finance at Your Finger Tips.

Tally ERP 9 price you pay, comes with rich features & financial reports that give you the necessary management and control of your business.

You will find a list of few of them below:

  • Funds Flow & Cash Flow reports – help you locate bottlenecks
  • Bank reconciliation – keeps your bank books in sync, and identify unclear instruments
  • Customer Credit Limits – to limit the risk of default and large losses
  • Budgets – keep tabs on projected expenses against actuals
  • Mark vouchers as post-dated – these will reflect into the books of accounts only on/after that date
  • We can mark vouchers (called ‘reversing journals’) to ‘disappear’ for reports post a specific date for needs where reports have to consider a transaction that has not yet taken place, (say salaries for this month that we have to pay the next month).
  • “Scenario” management – helps you with your business forecasting and planning. You can use optional, reversing journals and memorandum vouchers, to aid in recording provisional entries that are useful for interim reports
  • Multiple inventory valuation. You can decide a valuation method for the business, and generate financial reports using another valuation method – as the same method may not be appropriate for all your needs, your bankers and for other decisions
  • Tally warns you if cash balances go negative or if it is about to happen, or see reports and make the necessary corrections
  • Several business exceptions are available (negative stocks, negative ledgers) – for you to exercise control over these activities
  • A single-screen ratios report with a drill-down right up to the transactions – helps you to correct course frequently
  • Item and document wise profitability statements to control revenue leakage and inappropriate pricing
  • Tally software supports internal audits. With audit controls, authorized users can mark vouchers as ‘audited’ and can get to see reports on changes made post-audit, and the name of the person making the change.
